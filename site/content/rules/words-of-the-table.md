*A first glossary of the game's terms of art. Provisional — every entry here
yields to the handbook once it is bound.*

---

<dl>

<dt>Price &amp; Grace</dt>
<dd>The two ways every answer comes. What you want may be <em>seized at a
price</em> — paid for, openly — or <em>given as grace</em>, unearned. The game
never promises which.</dd>

<dt>The Three Clues</dt>
<dd>Behind every locked door wait three pre-written clues, so no soul is ever
truly stuck.</dd>

<dt>Standing</dt>
<dd>Goodwill with a people — a stake in the Ledger. Standing is won or wounded
a watch at a time, not taken in a scene.</dd>

<dt>The Ledger</dt>
<dd>Where Standing is kept.</dd>

<dt>Works</dt>
<dd>Undertakings declared in the open, with hands you can spare and power you
can pledge. The way Standing is built.</dd>

<dt>The Great Work</dt>
<dd>The largest undertaking a crew sets turning.</dd>

<dt>Divine Works</dt>
<dd>The tier above: a Work with no counted end and several storms sealed
inside it, each breaking in its season — some rewriting the very aim it began
with.</dd>

<dt>The Council Table — Wide or Narrow</dt>
<dd>The choice of who is seated when a thing is decided.</dd>

<dt>Minuted Dissent</dt>
<dd>Objection written into the record at the time of the decision — so that
when a cost falls, the objection is already written down.</dd>

<dt>Hope</dt>
<dd>The creed. The game can wound you, and grieve you, but it breaks only the
one who stops hoping.</dd>

</dl>
